# **2. Biosecurity and Biocontrol Functions of MBT55/HMT238

— An Ecosystem‑Level Defense System That Prevents Disease Establishment —**

MBT55/HMT238 is a **high‑diversity microbial consortium** composed of more than 120 species.  
It provides a level of **ecosystem biosecurity** that cannot be achieved with chemical pesticides, fertilizers, or single‑strain microbial products.

Its core principle is:

> **Instead of “killing pathogens,” MBT55/HMT238 creates an environment where pathogens cannot establish in the first place.**

This represents a shift from chemical agriculture to **ecological biosecurity**.

# **2.2 The Five‑Layer Biosecurity Model of MBT55/HMT238**

MBT55/HMT238 provides biosecurity through a **five‑layer defense system**.

---

## **Layer 1: Priority Effect (Early Occupation of Ecological Niches)**

MBT55/HMT238 rapidly colonizes leaf surfaces, bark, roots, and the rhizosphere,  
preventing pathogens from attaching, germinating, or invading.

### ✔ Mechanisms

- Biofilm formation
- Occupation of attachment sites
- Pre‑emptive nutrient consumption

### ✔ Target Diseases

- Coffee Leaf Rust (CLR)
- Anthracnose
- CBD
- Cercospora
- Pink Disease
- Shiitake Blue Mold
- Honeybee Chalkbrood

---

## **Layer 2: Antagonistic Metabolites**

MBT55/HMT238 produces **antifungal and antibacterial metabolites** that suppress pathogen growth.

### ✔ Key Metabolites

- Organic acids (lactic acid, acetic acid)
- Antimicrobial peptides
- Chitinase
- β‑1,3‑glucanase
- Volatile organic compounds (VOCs)

### ✔ Effects

- Inhibition of spore germination
- Inhibition of hyphal elongation
- Degradation of fungal cell walls

---

## **Layer 3: Micro‑environment Control**

Pathogens depend on specific micro‑environmental conditions (pH, humidity, nutrients, redox).  
MBT55/HMT238 modifies these conditions to eliminate pathogen niches.

### ✔ Mechanisms

- Stabilization of leaf surface pH
- Improved redox balance on bark surfaces
- Formation of soil aggregates
- Increased water‑holding capacity
- Fermentation‑dominant organic matter breakdown

### ✔ Effects

- Prevents CLR spore germination (requires leaf wetness)
- Inhibits Pink Disease hyphal invasion
- Reduces Cercospora toxin impact

---

## **Layer 4: Induced Systemic Resistance (ISR / ISE)**

MBT55/HMT238 activates plant immune pathways.

### ✔ Mechanisms

- Induction of PR proteins (PR‑1, PR‑2, PR‑3)
- Activation of phenolic metabolism
- Lignification of cell walls
- Callose deposition

### ✔ Effects

- Increased resistance to CLR, Anthracnose, CBD
- Stronger bark structure
- Higher resistance in young fruits

---

## **Layer 5: Ecosystem Feedback Loop**

MBT55/HMT238 creates a **positive ecological feedback cycle**:

> Microbes → Environmental improvement → Microbial dominance → Pathogen suppression

### ✔ Outcomes

- Soil fertility increases year by year
- Plant vigor improves
- Disease risk decreases
- Production costs decline

- Chitinase degraded fungal cell walls

**→ Strong evidence for application to coffee fungal diseases.**
