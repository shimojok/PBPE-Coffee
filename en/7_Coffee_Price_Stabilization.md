# **7.1 Five Root Causes of Coffee Price Volatility**

Coffee price volatility is driven by five interconnected structural factors.

---

## **① Climate Change (Unstable Yields)**

- Droughts
- Excessive rainfall
- Frosts
- Heatwaves
- Irregular seasons

These cause **sudden yield drops**, leading to price spikes.

---

## **② Plant Diseases (CLR, CBD, Anthracnose, Cercospora)**

Diseases can destroy **30–70%** of production.

Climate change expands disease distribution, increasing:

- CLR outbreaks
- CBD severity
- Anthracnose fruit drop
- Cercospora defoliation

---

## **③ Rising Production Costs**

- Fertilizer price spikes
- Agrochemical inflation
- Labor shortages
- Currency fluctuations (USD‑based trade)

High costs reduce farmer profitability → reduced supply → price instability.

---

## **④ Logistics & Geopolitical Risks**

- Red Sea route disruptions
- Panama Canal drought
- Rising fuel costs
- Regional conflicts

These increase transportation costs and reduce supply reliability.

---

## **⑤ Speculative Capital in Futures Markets**

Coffee is heavily traded on futures markets.  
Speculative flows amplify price swings.
