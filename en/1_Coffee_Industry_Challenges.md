# **1. Complex Challenges Facing the Global Coffee Industry

— Climate Change, Diseases, Soil Degradation, and Price Volatility —**

The global coffee industry is one of the agricultural sectors most vulnerable to climate change.  
It faces **multiple, simultaneous stressors** including plant diseases, soil degradation, rising production costs, labor shortages, and extreme price volatility.

This chapter provides a comprehensive overview of the structural challenges that PBPE × MBT55/HMT238 aims to solve.

---

# **1.1 Shrinking Coffee‑Suitable Areas Due to Climate Change (The 2050 Crisis)**

Leading research institutions (WCR, CIRAD, FAO) consistently project that  
**more than 50% of current Arabica‑suitable land will become unsuitable by 2050**.

### ■ Key Drivers

- Rising temperatures (beyond optimal range)
- Irregular rainfall patterns (droughts and floods)
- Extreme weather events (frosts, heatwaves)
- Soil moisture instability

### ■ Impacts

- Unstable yields
- Increased disease and pest pressure
- Upslope migration of coffee farms (risk of deforestation)
- Rising production costs

**→ The very survival of Arabica coffee is at risk.**

---

# **1.2 Intensifying Coffee Diseases (CLR, CBD, Anthracnose, Cercospora, Pink Disease)**

Climate change accelerates the spread and severity of major coffee diseases.

## **Major Coffee Diseases**

|Disease|Pathogen|Symptoms|Impact|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Coffee Leaf Rust (CLR)**|_Hemileia vastatrix_|Orange spores on leaf underside, defoliation|30–70% yield loss|
|**Anthracnose**|_Colletotrichum_ spp.|Black lesions on cherries, fruit drop|Quality loss, yield reduction|
|**Coffee Berry Disease (CBD)**|_C. kahawae_|Black rot on green cherries|Devastating in Africa|
|**Cercospora Leaf Spot**|_Cercospora_ spp.|Brown leaf spots, defoliation|Reduced vigor, next‑year yield loss|
|**Pink Disease**|_Erythricium salmonicolor_|Pink mycelium on bark, branch dieback|Tree death|

### ■ Structural Problems

- Heavy dependence on chemical fungicides (copper, systemic fungicides)
- Emergence of resistant strains
- Smallholders cannot afford adequate disease control
- Climate change expands disease distribution

**→ Coffee diseases are a “climate × microbiome × nutrition × plant vigor” problem,  
and chemical‑only approaches are no longer sufficient.**

---

# **1.3 Bark and Stem Disorders (Cankers, Borers, Physiological Damage)**

Coffee bark is essential for protecting the tree from pathogens, dehydration, and acid rain.  
However, recent years have seen:

- Bark peeling
- Cankers
- Borer damage
- Acid rain–induced bark deterioration

### ■ Causes

- Nutrient deficiencies (Ca, B, Si)
- Soil degradation
- Drought stress
- Secondary infections

**→ Bark disorders indicate a collapse of the plant’s immune system.**

---

# **1.4 Soil Degradation and Nutrient Imbalance**

Soil degradation is one of the most severe and widespread challenges in coffee‑producing regions.

### ■ Key Issues

- Loss of organic matter
- Declining microbial diversity
- Reduced water‑holding capacity
- Heavy dependence on chemical fertilizers
- Soil acidification
- Continuous cropping fatigue

### ■ Consequences

- Low seedling survival
- Weak plant vigor
- Higher susceptibility to diseases
- Unstable yields

**→ Soil degradation is the fundamental bottleneck of the coffee industry.**

---

# **1.5 Rising Production Costs (Fertilizers, Agrochemicals, Labor)**

Production costs have surged globally.

### ■ Cost Drivers

- Fertilizer price spikes (N, P, K)
- Rising agrochemical costs
- Labor shortages
- Increased logistics costs
- Currency fluctuations (USD‑based trade)

### ■ Impact on Smallholders

- Shrinking profit margins
- Reduced capacity to invest in disease control
- Increased vulnerability to climate shocks
- Higher rates of farm abandonment

**→ Economic fragility at the farm level destabilizes the entire supply chain.**

---

# **1.6 Coffee Price Volatility and Market Instability**

Coffee prices have experienced extreme fluctuations in recent years.

### ■ Three‑Layer Structure of Price Volatility

|Layer|Description|
|---|---|
|**Supply‑side shocks**|Droughts, frosts, disease outbreaks|
|**Demand‑side pressure**|Rapid growth in Asian markets|
|**External factors**|Currency shifts, logistics disruptions, geopolitical risks|

**→ Price volatility affects farmers, roasters, retailers, and consumers alike.**

---

# **1.7 Coffee Grounds Waste Problem (Environmental Burden & Cost)**

Millions of tons of spent coffee grounds (SCG) are generated annually.

### ■ Current Problems

- High moisture → heavy → expensive to transport
- High disposal and incineration costs
- CO₂ emissions from burning
- Regulatory barriers to reuse
- Waste costs burden cafés and roasters

**→ Coffee grounds are an untapped resource requiring a circular solution.**

---

# **1.8 Summary: The Coffee Industry Faces a “Polycrisis”**

The challenges facing coffee are **complex, interconnected, and structural**:

- Climate change
- Plant diseases
- Soil degradation
- Rising production costs
- Price volatility
- Waste management issues

These factors reinforce each other, creating a **systemic crisis** that cannot be solved with traditional inputs alone.
